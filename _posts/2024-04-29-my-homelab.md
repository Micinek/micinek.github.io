# How, Why and What?!

Ever feel that itch to experiment with technology, but want to start small? A homelab is like dipping your toe in the pond – a perfect way to test the waters without going all in.

You can start small, and that's the beauty of a homelab! It can be whatever you want it to be. People create homelabs for many reasons, from building practical skills for their careers, or have fun tinkering with new software. It's a way to learn, explore, and unleash your inner Wesley Crusher. Let's dive into my homelab and see what kind of adventures await!

I started to think about homelab soon after i started at my job as tech support. I had to support end users and later windows servers, i did not had the oportunity to get some big course to learn it all, we had only a few people as small company, but my boss taught me a lot, also my father that was working with computers a lot helped me to become better at my work.

My boss supported my curiosity, so i could take any old, unused server that came our way and tinker with it. My homelab was born. It went through a LOT of changes, starting with diferent cases, going back and forth with Xeon and desktop CPUs, SAS drives vs SATA, later SSDs when they began to be afordable.

Every homelab is unique, ever changing ecosystem of anything and everything you need. My homelab is currently the biggest homelab i want to afford in terms of power consumption. My homelab rack is currently drawing about 140W. Since the electricity prices are not realy pleasant right now, I dont want to run 1kW+ homelab 24/7. So i run few low power computers, more on that in my next posts, including what i run on those servers.

I learned from a lot of people on forums, blogs, youtube. And I hope, I can bring my own insight to homelabing, by sharing my journey and help you with yours.
